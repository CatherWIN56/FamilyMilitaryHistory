Douglas Bernardy

Early Life

Douglas, commonly known as Doug, was born on May 15, 1956, in Marshall, Minnesota, but primarily resided in the quaint town of Minneota, Minnesota. As the third child among four boys and one girl, with his sister being the youngest, Doug grew up in a vibrant household. Throughout his formative years, he displayed a penchant for athleticism and engagement in various extracurricular activities, including baseball, swimming, diving, roller skating, wrestling, and dancing. Renowned for his sociability and affable demeanor, Doug enjoyed a notable level of popularity during his high school years. His academic journey unfolded within the confines of local educational institutions, wherein he spent the entirety of his childhood in Minneota, fostering strong ties to his community.

Joining Up

At the age of 17, Doug made a decisive career choice to enlist in the United States Navy, a decision that entailed leaving high school prematurely. With the supportive consent of his parents, Doug embarked on his military journey, epitomizing a resolute commitment to service. During his tenure in the Navy, he pursued his educational advancement by obtaining his GED while fulfilling his duties as a hull technician. As an integral member of the damage control team, Doug played a pivotal role in training fellow servicemen in damage control procedures. His responsibilities extended to conducting nondestructive testing, employing techniques such as ultrasounds and x-rays to assess the integrity of welds. Engaging in hazardous tasks involving radiation, Doug adhered to stringent safety protocols, including the utilization of radiation-tracking devices. Stationed aboard a ship in San Diego, CA, Doug's service took him to the waters adjacent to Vietnam during the concluding stages of the war. Notably, he participated in clandestine operations, including a covert land mission aimed at facilitating the evacuation of military personnel from Vietnam, showcasing his versatility and valor in the face of adversity.

Career

Following the completion of his four-year contract with the United States Navy, Doug transitioned to the Minnesota Army National Guard, concurrently embarking on a new chapter in his personal life by marrying Gina Flood. During his tenure in the National Guard, Doug and Gina welcomed two children, Angela and Nick. However, amidst economic challenges in 1982, Doug faced the daunting prospect of supporting his growing family, prompting his decision to reenlist in the Navy.

Stationed aboard the USS Ranger in San Diego, CA, Doug found himself confronting perilous circumstances when a devastating fire erupted onboard while the ship was traversing the Indian Ocean. As part of a courageous team, Doug played a pivotal role in containing the fire, safeguarding the vessel and its crew. Amidst the chaos, Doug's unwavering commitment to his family remained steadfast, providing support to Gina as they navigated the challenges of relocation and adaptation to their new surroundings.

Throughout his extensive naval career, Doug demonstrated exceptional professionalism and resilience, consistently rising to the occasion in the face of adversity. From San Diego, CA, Doug's assignments took him on a whirlwind journey across various naval bases and deployments. After the fire incident on the USS Ranger, Doug and his family were relocated to Bremerton, Washington, where the aircraft carrier was refitted. Subsequently, Doug underwent non-destructive testing school in San Diego, before being stationed at Naval Submarine Base Bangor, Washington, for three years. Following this, a two-year assignment in Guam first on the USS Proteus. This offered a unique experience of serving on a submarine tender. Doug followed this ship back to the states to be decomissioned in which time his family back on guam endured the trials of a significant typhoon, the largest to hit the island in a long while and an 8.3 sized earthquake.

Doug's career trajectory then led him back to Guam for another stint on the USS Holland, further enriching his naval experience. Despite his desire to be stationed at Great Lakes, IL, the Navy had other plans, initially intending to send him to Japan. However, last-minute orders rerouted him to the San Diego Naval Base, where he undertook teaching duties in non-destructive testing. The eventual closure of the school prompted another relocation, this time to Great Lakes, IL, where Doug continued his teaching responsibilities until his retirement decision. Throughout his military journey, Doug's steadfast dedication to his family, unwavering professionalism, and adaptability in the face of shifting assignments and challenges underscored his distinguished naval career.

Retirement

Following his retirement from the Navy in 1999, Doug transitioned to civilian employment, securing a position at MQS in Milwaukee where he specialized in nondestructive testing for a duration of five years. Subsequently, he advanced to MetalTek International, assuming the role of supervisor and radiation safety officer for nondestructive testing operations. Doug's tenure at Medal Tech spanned an impressive 18 years, during which he found fulfillment in teaching and guiding his colleagues while exhibiting a steadfast dedication to his profession. Unfortunately, his career was interrupted by a stroke in 2018, compelling him to retire.

Doug and his wife, Gina, had already made plans to relocate to Mount Zion, IL, to be closer to their daughter, Angela, and her family, as their son, serving in the Active-duty Army, experienced frequent relocations. This strategic decision ensured consistency and familial support during their retirement years. Settling into their new home, Doug cherished his retirement, enjoying precious moments with his beloved dog, Eddie, indulging in a purchase of a VW convertable, and relishing quality time with Gina and their cherished grandchildren. Throughout his retirement years, Doug remained a devoted family man, finding joy and fulfillment in the company of his loved ones.

On Wednesday, February 7, 2024, Douglas departed this world, leaving behind a legacy of love, strength, and unwavering commitment to his family and community.
