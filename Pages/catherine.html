Catherine Clark

Early Life

Catherine was born in Kenosha, WI on November 29th, 1999. She began attending school at two years old at Big Apple Early Learning Academy. One she was of age, she began her preschool studies at Bose Elementary school, she continued there until she was transferred to Somers Elementary School due to the redrawing of school district lines. At 9 years old, she and her family moved to Mount Zion, IL due to her step-father, Christopher, joining the AGR program for the Army National Guard. She began studying at Mount Zion Intermediate school and continued her education in Mount Zion School District until she graduated in 2018.

Joining Up

While sitting in her ACT/SAT Prep Focus Period her junior year of high school, her teacher at the time had assigned the class to practice writing college essays. Catherine, having no plans to attend college, was watching YouTube videos. Her teacher asked her if she planned on flipping burgers for the rest of her life. She, after looking down and seeing an ad to join the military, replied saying she was going to join the Army. The teacher then stated that she would never make it through Basic Training. This sparked something in her to prove that teacher wrong. She spoke to a recruiter that night and was raising her right hand and signing the dotted line just two weeks later. She attended Basic Training following her high school graduation, which she not only passed, but was promoted ahead of peers due to good performance.

Career

Catherine embarked on her journey at Fort Gordon, now Fort Eisenhower, GA, with the aspiration to become an IT Specialist, earning her job code of 25B. Her dedication and skill propelled her through her initial assignment with the 419th Mobilization Control Battalion (MCB), where she swiftly rose through the ranks, outpacing her peers.

Deploying with the battalion during the Afghanistan retrograde in support of Operation Enduring Freedom (Spartan Shielf), she assumed the additional responsibility of Contracting Officer Representative (COR), managing vast sums of contract funds crucial to mission success.

Upon returning stateside, she transitioned to the 393 PSYOP Company, achieving the rank of Sergeant (SGT) and honing her skills at JRTC, Fort Polk, now Fort Johnson, LA.

Continuing her pursuit of excellence, Catherine contracted with ROTC at Marquette University, leading to her transfer to the 16th PSYOP BN. Set to commission on May 11th, 2024, as a Field Artillery Officer with a basic branch of Signal Corps, Catherine exemplifies a remarkable journey marked by unwavering commitment and a relentless pursuit of advancement.

Future

Much of Catherine's future is unknown. She will be attending Basic Officer Leadership Course (BOLC) at some point in the future at Fort Sill, Oklahoma. While there she will recieve her orders for her first duty station. Once promoted to Captain (CPT) she will be attending Captain's Career Course (CCC) at Fort Eisenhower, Georgia to become a Signal Corps Officer. She plans to do the full 20 years for retirement.
