Christopher Thunder

Early Life

Born on June 2nd, 1977, in Chicago, Christopher's journey through education took him from various suburbs in the Chicagoland area, including Bolingbrook and Gurnee, before settling in Lake Forest/Lake Bluff in 1991 for his 8th-grade year. Graduating from high school in 1995, he continued his academic pursuit at the College of Lake County in Grayslake, IL, where he obtained an Associate Degree in Arts in 1998. Christopher then ventured further south to Daytona Beach, FL, home to the renowned Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University, often dubbed the "Harvard of the Skies" (lol), where he earned his Bachelor of Science degree in Aeronautical Science in May 2001. With his educational achievements under his belt, Christopher was poised to take flight into the aviation industry, equipped with knowledge and determination to make a significant impact.

Joining Up

Motivated by a profound sense of duty and a desire to serve his country in the aftermath of the tragic events of September 11, 2001, Christopher made a pivotal decision to join the United States Army Reserve on November 19 of the same year. Enlisting as a 46Q, a Public Affairs, Media Relations, and Photo/Journalism Specialist, he became an integral part of the 416th Theatre Engineering Command based in Darien, IL. Despite his unwavering commitment to his nation, Christopher made the deeply personal choice not to pursue active duty, recognizing the importance of remaining close to his two children, Michelle and Alyssa, at the time. His decision to serve in the Reserve underscored his dedication to both his country and his family, embodying the selflessness and sacrifice of a true patriot.

Career

Despite his background in aviation, Christopher's desire to serve his country in a different capacity led him to explore alternative career paths. Intent on pursuing a career in journalism, he encountered unexpected challenges with a newspaper scandal that halted his plans. Undeterred, he embarked on a journey with the military, where he found purpose and fulfillment. His dedication to serving his country remained steadfast, even as personal milestones intersected with his military career. While on an exercise with the 416th in Panama, Christopher received news that he and his partner Angela were expecting a child, Dylan, adding a new dimension to his sense of responsibility.

As the surge in Iraq unfolded in 2004-2005, Christopher faced pivotal decisions regarding his military career. Though he initially aspired to pursue a commission as a Regular Line Officer (RLO), he ultimately opted to become a Warrant Officer, drawn by the unique opportunities and responsibilities it offered. Navigating through bureaucratic hurdles, Christopher's determination and resourcefulness propelled him forward. His journey took him to flight school from 2007-2009, where his exceptional performance earned him a full-time National Guard position flying for counter-drug and homeland security units in Decatur. During his time at flight school, Christopher recieved news that he and his partner Angela were expecting their second child together, Dalton.

Throughout his military career, Christopher's partnership with Angela remained a source of strength and support. Their marriage in 2008 in Dothan, AL, marked a new chapter in their lives, prompting a relocation to Decatur following Christopher's return from flight school and the ~offical~ gaining of his step-daughter, Catherine. During his time at the unit in Decatur, Christopher was offered the oppornity to deploy to Saudi Arabia during the height of the Afghanistan Retrograde in 2021 and 2022, he was located there for 15 months, with a short journey home in Mount Zion, IL for the holiday, returning to Saudi Arabia to finish out his 15 months. Christopher's journey epitomizes the sacrifices and triumphs of military service, underscored by his unwavering commitment to his family and his nation.

Future

Looking ahead to the future, Christopher harbors aspirations of transitioning to fixed-wing aircraft, a goal he has long pursued. Despite encountering obstacles in Decatur due to entrenched social dynamics, Christopher's determination remained unwavering. Following his tenure as a Battalion Aviation Element (BAE) for the Joint Readiness Training Center (JRTC), he made a strategic move, transferring to the United States Army Reserve (USAR) to fly MEDEVAC missions, aiming to strike a balance between his military career and personal life, particularly as he was also flying for commercial airlines at the time. However, unforeseen challenges emerged with the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, disrupting his plans to qualify for UH-60M and undergo MEDEVAC training. Faced with adversity, Christopher embarked on a new chapter by accepting opportunities abroad, applying for positions with C12 and JetBlue while stationed in Saudi Arabia. His perseverance and adaptability ultimately paid off when he received acceptance offers from both airlines on the same day, signaling a promising trajectory for his future endeavors in aviation.
